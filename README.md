 The 11 Sectors of the Stock Market
The stock market is typically divided into 11 sectors under the Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS).
These group companies by primary business activities for diversification.

1. Energy: Oil, gas, renewable energy, equipment/services
2. Materials: Chemicals, metals, mining, construction materials, forestry/paper
3. Industrials: Aerospace/defense, construction, machinery, transportation, logistics
4. Consumer Discretionary: Autos, apparel, leisure, hotels, restaurants, retail
5. Consumer Staples: Food/beverages, tobacco, household products, essential retailers
6. Health Care: Biotech, pharmaceuticals, health services, medical devices
7. Financials: Banks, insurance, REITs, financial services
8. Information Technology: Software, hardware, semiconductors, IT services
9. Communication Services: Telecom, media/entertainment, interactive media
10. Utilities: Electric, gas, water, multi-utilities, independent power producers
11. Real Estate: Equity REITs, real estate management/development
